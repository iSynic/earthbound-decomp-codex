**Scope**

This thread shifted from decomp into patch-first investigation of EarthBound’s overworld walking microstutter. We are no longer trying to stay ROM-first here; refs and decomp notes are in play. The current state of the work is captured mainly in [rom-patch-overworld-stutter-plan.md](F:/Earthbound%20Decomp%20-%20Codex/notes/rom-patch-overworld-stutter-plan.md), [overworld-walking-stutter-producer-split-c01558-c01ca8.md](F:/Earthbound%20Decomp%20-%20Codex/notes/overworld-walking-stutter-producer-split-c01558-c01ca8.md), and [overworld-timer-rerun-gate-a6e3-a794.md](F:/Earthbound%20Decomp%20-%20Codex/notes/overworld-timer-rerun-gate-a6e3-a794.md).

**Big Picture**

The original theory was “walking stutter is mostly tile/strip loading pressure.” That was partly right, but incomplete. The strongest current model is that the remaining hitch is caused by multiple legitimate movement-time visual update families piling up in the same frame, especially during diagonal scrolling.

Two producer families are now clearly implicated:
- The strip/tilemap family centered on `C0:0E16` and `C0:0FCB`
- A companion visual/entity family centered upstream on `C0:255C/25CF/2A6B/2B55`, then `C0:2A50 -> C0:2957 -> C0:1E49`, and later `C0:1CA8` plus `C0:A4C4/A794`

That split is the most important decomp result so far.

**What We Now Understand Well**

The queue and copy path is much better understood:
- `C0:8616` is the strongest current fit for the generic `TRANSFER_TO_VRAM` path.
- `C0:865F` is the key queue/immediate helper under it.
- `C0:8240` is the NMI-side DMA queue consumer.
- The queue is descriptor-based at WRAM `$0400+`, with 8-byte descriptors.

The meaning of the scratch fields around `C0:865F..869D` is now much clearer:
- `$92` is the transfer size in bytes
- `$97` is the VRAM destination word
- `$99` is the cumulative queued transfer-byte total for the current frame
- `Y` is the descriptor write cursor

That correction mattered a lot, because it fixed some earlier mistaken assumptions about what the emulator samples meant.

**Movement Refresh Structure**

`C0:1558` is still the strongest current fit for the incremental movement/scroll refresh controller. It splits naturally into two axis lanes:
- Horizontal lane: `C0:0BDC`, `C0:0D7E`, `C0:0FCB`, `C0:25CF`, `C0:2B55`
- Vertical lane: `C0:0AC5`, `C0:0CF3`, `C0:0E16`, `C0:255C`, `C0:2A6B`

This gives a very straightforward explanation for why diagonal scrolling looks worst: both movement-refresh lanes can do work in the same overall update.

**Strip/Tilemap Family Findings**

The map-strip side is still real and important:
- `C0:0E16` and `C0:0FCB` are the strongest current strip upload helpers.
- They allocate scratch through `C0:86DE`, build payloads, then call `C0:8616`.
- Their outputs align with the expected tilemap VRAM ranges:
  - `38xx`
  - `3Cxx`
  - `58xx`
  - `5Cxx`

Observed busy-frame samples like `58A0`, `5A60`, `5B60`, `5C20`, and `5E60` fit this family very well.

**Companion Visual/Entity Family Findings**

The second producer family is the newer and more important discovery:
- `C0:255C/25CF/2A6B/2B55` are movement-adjacent companion producers, not no-op cosmetics.
- They feed `C0:2A50 -> C0:2957 -> C0:1E49`.
- `C0:1E49` seeds per-entry tile bases into `$298E` using `C4:2F8C + #$4000`.
- That directly supports observed VRAM destinations like:
  - `4040`
  - `4120`
  - `42A0`

Two downstream paths then make this family visible:
- A narrow direct uploader at `C0:1CA8..1D37`, locally proving `0x4000 + offset` and `0x4100 + offset`
- A broader descriptor family at `C0:A4C4` and `C0:A794`, which eventually queue through `C0:A56E -> C0:8643 -> C0:8677`

This is the strongest explanation for the sampled `40xx/41xx/42xx` VRAM family.

**What We Proved About A4C4 / A794**

Both `C0:A4C4` and `C0:A794` are real descriptor producers over the companion visual lane:
- Both load transfer size from `$2A7E`
- Both load VRAM base from `$298E`
- Both consume a descriptor stream rooted in `$29CA`
- Both feed `C0:A56E`, which can split one logical update into two queued descriptors if a `0x0100` VRAM page boundary is crossed

The important split between them is:
- `A4C4` is the simpler selector-driven producer using `DATA_C0A60B` and `$2892`
- `A794` is the active-entry, phase-biased producer using `DATA_C0A623`, `$2896`, and `$10F2`

`$10F2` now looks like a small per-entry phase or variant offset. `A794` is the more likely “walking-time amplifier” because it is periodically rerun by the watcher at `C0:A6E3..A753`.

**What We Tried as Actual Patches**

The first real patch was the strip dedupe patch:
- [overworld_stutter_dedupe_asar_sketch.asm](F:/Earthbound%20Decomp%20-%20Codex/asm/overworld_stutter_dedupe_asar_sketch.asm)
- stable baseline ROM: [EarthBound-overworld-stutter-safe-v2.sfc](F:/Earthbound%20Decomp%20-%20Codex/build/EarthBound-overworld-stutter-safe-v2.sfc)

This patch hooks the eight overworld-only `JSL C08616` strip upload callsites, caches 64-byte payloads, and skips exact duplicates. After fixing lazy runtime init, it became stable.

User result:
- no crashes
- maybe somewhat smoother
- single-axis scrolling seems smoother
- diagonal microstutters remain

That was an important success, but also showed strip redundancy is only part of the problem.

The second conservative patch experiment was the timer-only `A794` gate:
- [overworld_stutter_timer_gate_overlay.asm](F:/Earthbound%20-Decomp%20-%20Codex/asm/overworld_stutter_timer_gate_overlay.asm)
- built test ROM: [EarthBound-overworld-stutter-safe-v3-timer-gate.sfc](F:/Earthbound%20Decomp%20-%20Codex/build/EarthBound-overworld-stutter-safe-v3-timer-gate.sfc)

This patch hooked only the timer-expiry rerun leg at `C0:A723..A750`, derived the post-toggle raw header `A794` would use, compared it against `$341A`, and skipped the timer-only rerun if the header matched.

User result:
- no crashes
- no meaningful improvement in visible microstutter

That negative result matters a lot: it suggests the timer-only `A794` rerun path is not a dominant contributor to the visible hitch.

**What We Learned From Stable Telemetry**

The most useful stable telemetry builds were:
- [EarthBound-overworld-stutter-debug-v2.sfc](F:/Earthbound%20Decomp%20-%20Codex/build/EarthBound-overworld-stutter-debug-v2.sfc)
- [EarthBound-overworld-stutter-scheduler-debug-v2.sfc](F:/Earthbound%20Decomp%20-%20Codex/build/EarthBound-overworld-stutter-scheduler-debug-v2.sfc)

From those, the user observed:
- Vertical and horizontal eligible strip counters both climb during diagonal scrolling.
- Strip-skip counters stay near zero in that case.
- `F16A`, the queued-byte total sampled before reset, stays modest in calm cases and rises during diagonal scrolling.
- Microstutters still happen even when the earlier strip counters are not moving much.

Interpretation:
- diagonal scrolling causes real work on both axes
- the remaining issue correlates better with aggregate queue/load pressure than with missed strip dedupe alone

**What We Ruled Out**

One tempting theory was that the visible hitch was caused by the explicit queue-overflow spin-wait at `C0:8671..8673`. That no longer looks right.

Evidence:
- On the stable patched baseline, a breakpoint at `C0:8671` did not fire during visible stutter.
- Claude’s later handoff says the same thing appears true on vanilla ROM too.

That means the remaining visible hitch is probably not the literal “queue hit `$1200`, main thread spun waiting for NMI” case.

We also rejected a couple of invasive debug builds:
- `scheduler-debug-v3` destabilized rendering and produced corruption
- `scheduler-debug-v5` made even menus lag badly
- those builds should not be trusted for conclusions

**What Emulator Sampling Showed**

The best low-level emulator samples around `C0:8677` showed:
- repeated `0x40`-byte transfers
- busy-frame destinations clustering into two families:
  - strip-like `58xx/5Axx/5Bxx/5Cxx/5Exx`
  - companion-like `4040/4120/42A0`

This is one of the strongest empirical results we have. The busy frames are not one giant transfer and not one oddball path. They are multiple normal 64-byte updates landing in a few destination families.

**Current Confidence Levels**

High confidence:
- The strip family is real.
- The companion visual/entity family is real.
- Both families can be active during walking.
- Diagonal movement is structurally the worst case because both axis lanes can contribute.
- The timer-only `A794` rerun is safe to gate, but not impactful enough to explain the remaining stutter.

Medium confidence:
- The remaining microstutter is an aggregate per-frame workload problem rather than a single bad routine.
- `A794` contributes, but is probably not the dominant remaining cause by itself.

Low confidence:
- That another `A794`-only patch will solve the problem.
- That the remaining hitch is primarily “loading” rather than broader movement/display pacing.

**Claude’s Latest Strongest Hypothesis**

Claude’s latest handoff adds one important alternative explanation:
- the queue may not be overflowing, but the main loop may still sometimes take longer than one NMI period during diagonal movement
- in that model, the stutter comes from missing an expected scroll commit rather than from queue saturation

I think that is a serious hypothesis now, not a fringe idea. It fits several observations:
- diagonal is worst
- cutscene camera panning and diagonal walking look similar
- safe-v2 helps a bit by removing some CPU work
- scheduler experiments help somewhat by reducing two-axis work

That hypothesis is not fully proved in this thread yet, but it is now one of the leading explanations.

**Bottom Line**

We started by chasing tile loading. We now know the real situation is more complex.

The strongest current summary is:
- There are at least two legitimate movement-time producer families.
- The strip family was worth patching, and the dedupe patch helped somewhat.
- The remaining visible microstutter does not seem to be the explicit queue-overflow spin-wait.
- The timer-only `A794` branch is safe to gate but was not important enough to matter visibly.
- The dominant remaining problem is likely broader per-frame movement/render workload, especially when both axis lanes contribute during diagonal motion.

So we have made substantial progress in understanding the system and in ruling things out, but we do not yet have a strong final fix for the residual microstutter.
