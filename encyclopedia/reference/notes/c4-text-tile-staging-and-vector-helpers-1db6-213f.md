## Text tile staging helper

`C4:1DB6` writes masked words into the same WRAM tile-staging area used by `C4:002F` (`$3492`, `$34A2`, and `$34B2` plus an offset in `$0A`). It derives row/column components from `$3C14`, `$3C16`, `$3C18`, and `$3C1C`, calls the local renderer helper at `C4:1BCA`, then merges generated bytes from `$3B12` into the staging rows.

The merge shape is consistent and important:

```text
new = ((generated_word ^ old_word) & mask) ^ old_word
```

That means the mask tables at `C4:1EB9`, `C4:1EC9`, and `C4:1ED9` gate which bits of the existing staged tile row get replaced. The helper also updates a low/high dirty range through `C4:1EE9` and `C4:1EF4`.

Given the surrounding text tile transfer work in `C4:002F` and the byte source at `$3B12`, the best current name is a masked glyph/text-tile staging renderer. The exact caller is not a direct `JSL`/`JSR` in the current scan, so this may be table-dispatched or reached through a local internal path.

## Dirty range helpers

`C4:1EE9` compares the current staging offset `$0A` against `$3C1E` and stores it when the offset is lower. `C4:1EF4` compares `$0A` against `$3C20` and stores it when the offset is higher or equal.

Together they maintain the minimum and maximum dirty offsets for the text tile staging buffer.

## Direction and vector helpers

`C4:1EFF` is the direction-from-delta helper already seen by the C0 pathfinding and NPC attention notes. It takes a coordinate pair/delta setup from callers, normalizes absolute deltas, uses a threshold table at `C4:1FDF`, and combines that with quadrant base values from `C4:1FC5` to return a direction/octant-style value.

`C4:213F` performs the fractional multiply used by that projection: it computes the high-byte-scaled product of an 8-bit coefficient and a 16-bit magnitude, effectively returning `(coefficient * magnitude) >> 8`.
